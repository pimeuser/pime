Industries We Serve

Australian silica and quartz minerals are essential inputs across a wide spectrum of industrial processes. Here's how PIME supports key sectors globally.

🏗️

Glass Manufacturing

Silica sand is the primary raw material in all glass production — from flat and float glass to container glass, fibreglass insulation, and specialty optical glass. Precise purity and grain consistency are critical to achieving clarity, strength, and uniformity. PIME's silica sand meets the stringent purity thresholds required by global glass producers, with low iron content to ensure colour fidelity in clear glass applications.

Silica Sand (99.5%+) High-Purity Quartz Custom Fine Grades
⚙️

Metal Casting & Foundry

Foundry-grade silica sand is used to create moulds and cores for metal casting in the automotive, aerospace, and heavy engineering sectors. The sand must exhibit precise thermal stability, good binding properties, and consistent sizing. PIME supplies foundry-grade silica with controlled grain fineness numbers (GFN) to match specific casting requirements.

Foundry Silica Sand Controlled GFN Grades
🏗️

Construction & Infrastructure

Silica is a key material in concrete, cement, mortar, render, and many construction composites. It is also used for sport surfaces, playgrounds, golf courses, and landscaping. Our construction-grade silica provides the durability and consistency demanded by large-scale infrastructure projects across Asia-Pacific and beyond.

Industrial Silica Bulk Construction Sand Graded Aggregates
🔋

Solar Energy & Electronics

The photovoltaic (solar panel) and semiconductor industries demand ultra-pure quartz as a critical feedstock. High-purity quartz (HPQ) is used to produce quartz crucibles, diffusion tubes, and other components in chip fabrication and solar cell production. PIME's HPQ products meet the ultra-low trace element specifications required for these demanding technology applications.

High-Purity Quartz (HPQ) 99.9%+ SiO₂ Grade
🎨

Paints, Coatings & Adhesives

Micronised quartz powder acts as a functional filler and extender in paints, industrial coatings, sealants, and adhesives. It improves scratch resistance, reduces shrinkage, enhances viscosity, and reduces cost while maintaining performance. Our quartz powders are available in multiple fineness grades to optimise coating formulations across architectural and protective applications.

Quartz Powder (fine grade) Ultra-fine Micronised
🧪

Ceramics & Refractories

Quartz and silica are essential raw materials in the production of ceramic tiles, sanitaryware, technical ceramics, and high-temperature refractory products. Consistent chemical purity and controlled particle size are essential to firing behaviour and final product quality. PIME supplies ceramic-grade quartz powder suited to both traditional and technical ceramic manufacturing.

Ceramic-Grade Quartz Powder Refractory Silica
💧

Water Treatment & Filtration

Graded silica sand and gravel are widely used in municipal and industrial water filtration systems. The uniform sizing and chemical inertness of silica make it ideal as a filter media for removing suspended solids from water. PIME can supply graded filter sand in various mesh sizes to suit different filtration system specifications and flow rates.

Filter Silica Sand Graded Filter Media
⛽

Oil & Gas (Frac Sand)

Hydraulic fracturing (fracking) uses large volumes of high-strength silica sand — known as frac sand or proppant — to prop open fractures in rock formations and allow oil and gas to flow. PIME can supply round-grain, crush-resistant silica sand meeting API frac sand specifications for oil and gas operations.

Frac Sand API-Grade Silica Proppant Grades
🔩

Rubber, Plastics & Composites

Silica and quartz powder are important reinforcing fillers in rubber compounding, thermoplastics, and fibre-reinforced composites. They enhance tensile strength, abrasion resistance, and dimensional stability. Our fine-grade quartz powders integrate seamlessly into polymer matrices for automotive parts, industrial hoses, and specialty materials.

Quartz Powder (fine) Surface-Treated Grades
